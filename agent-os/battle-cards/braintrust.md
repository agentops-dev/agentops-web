# Battle Card: Braintrust
*Last updated: 2026-03-30*

## At a Glance
- **What they do:** AI observability and evaluation platform — traces, automated evals, monitoring, experiment management for production AI applications
- **Founded:** 2023 | **Funding:** $121M total (Series B $80M, ICONIQ, a16z, Greylock) | **Team:** 123
- **Price:** Free (1M spans) → $249/mo Pro → Custom Enterprise
- **Model:** Per span + data storage (GB)
- **Best for:** AI-native companies (Notion, Replit, Cloudflare, Dropbox) needing enterprise-grade observability

## Their Strengths (be honest)
- Best-funded pure-play AI observability platform ($121M, $800M valuation) [Data]
- Impressive customer list: Notion, Replit, Cloudflare, Ramp, Dropbox, Vercel, Navan [Data]
- 123-person team builds fast
- AWS Marketplace listing creates enterprise distribution leverage [Data]
- "Observability layer for AI" positioning at infrastructure level is defensible
- ICONIQ + a16z + Greylock investor pedigree signals exit potential (IPO or acquisition)

## Their Weaknesses (your openings)
- **Most expensive Pro tier in category** ($249/mo vs. LangSmith $39/seat, Langfuse $29/mo) [Data]
- **Not open source** — data sovereignty concerns; no self-hosting below Enterprise
- **Zero coding agent management** — doesn't touch Claude Code, Cursor, Codex workflow
- **No organizational hierarchy** — monitoring tool focused on what happened, not what should happen
- **Competing directly with LangSmith and Langfuse** for the same enterprise buyers

## How to Win Against Them
- **When they say "Braintrust is our observability standard":** "Braintrust handles production monitoring. Agent OS handles organizational management. They're not competing — but if you're managing engineering teams using AI coding agents, Braintrust doesn't help with that workflow."
- **Lead with:** "Braintrust is for your production AI applications. Agent OS is for your development team's AI agent workflow. Different layer, different value."

## When They Win Over You
- **Large AI-native companies needing enterprise-grade observability** — this is exactly Braintrust's market; don't compete here
- **Teams needing automated evaluation pipelines** — Braintrust's eval capabilities are strong
- **Teams already embedded in AWS ecosystem** — Marketplace listing makes procurement easy

## Their Customers' Top Complaint
DATA GAP — insufficient review data found. High pricing ($249/mo Pro) is the most likely friction point based on pricing comparison. [Estimate]

## Key Vulnerability
Competing in a crowded observability market against LangSmith (also well-funded) and Langfuse (open-source). Being squeezed from below (price competition) and above (LangSmith enterprise dominance) is a structural challenge.

## Watch For
$80M Series B (Feb 2026) means they're building aggressively. If they expand scope beyond observability into the coding agent management space, they have resources to move fast. Low probability short-term given enterprise focus, but monitor quarterly.
